Disclosing Generative AI (GenAI) Use in Digital Humanities Research

Welcome to our study! The following page provides an overview of the study and outlines your rights as a participant.

Study Information and Statement of Informed Consent

STUDY NUMBER: IRB #: 24978

Overview: In this survey, we investigate the perceptions and approaches of digital humanists to disclosing the use of Generative AI (GenAI) in digital humanities research. The survey focuses on digital humanists in both the US and China.

Significance of the study: This study will provide important and unique evidence for academics who are taking a leadership role in the design of AI disclosure policies in academic institutions, funding agencies, and other organizations.

Approximate time to complete the survey: 10-15 minutes

The survey has three sections:

Part 1: About You. This section asks you to answer questions about yourself, particularly your research experiences in the digital humanities.

Part 2: GenAI Disclosure Across Research Contexts and Activities. This section asks you to answer questions about your attitudes and preferred approaches to GenAI disclosure across research contexts and activities.

Part 3: Your GenAI Literacy and Use. This section asks you to answer questions that measure your GenAI literacy and use.

VOLUNTARY NATURE OF PARTICIPATION: Your participation in this study is voluntary. You can leave at any point even after approving the consent form. It is important to note that you should be at least 18 years old to participate in the study.

PAY / COMPENSATION: You will not be paid for participating in this study.

DATA ACCESS: Your original responses will be accessible across two universities participating in the study: Indiana University Bloomington and the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. De-identified data will also be shared with the co-PI at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln.

POTENTIAL RISKS: You may be uncomfortable while answering some of the survey questions. While completing the survey, you can skip any questions that make you uncomfortable or that you do not want to answer. There is a risk that your responses could lose confidentiality, potentially impacting your reputation, educational progress, or employment. However, the research team will take every precaution to de-identify collected responses and store all data securely in encrypted devices.

QUESTIONS & CONTACT: If you have any questions about the study, please contact Rongqian Ma (Indiana University Bloomington) at rm56@iu.edu.

For questions about your rights as a research participant; to discuss problems, complaints, or concerns about a research study; or to obtain information or to offer input, please contact the Indiana University Human Research Protection Program office at 800-696-2949 or at irb@iu.edu.
