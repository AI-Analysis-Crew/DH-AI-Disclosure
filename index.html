Abstract

This survey study investigates how digital humanists perceive and approach generative AI (GenAI) disclosure in research. The results indicate that while digital humanities scholars acknowledge the importance of disclosing GenAI use, the actual rate of disclosure in research practice remains low. Respondents differ in their views on which activities most require disclosure and on the most appropriate methods for doing so. Most also believe that safeguards for AI disclosure should be established through institutional policies rather than left to individual decisions. Findings

The survey study reveals a shared recognition of the importance of disclosing GenAI use, but divergent views on how such disclosure should be integrated into research practices.
